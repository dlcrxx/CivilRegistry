What should I do if there is a typo or error in my PSA document?

If you notice an error (such as a misspelled name, wrong birthdate, or incorrect gender), you need to file a correction at the Local Civil Registry (LCR) where the event was registered. PSA only issues documents based on the LCR's official records.

Can I request a correction through eSertipiko Marikina?

No. eSertipiko Marikina only processes requests for copies of PSA documents. It is not authorized to handle corrections or amendments. To correct errors such as misspelled names, wrong dates, or other discrepancies, you must personally visit your Local Civil Registry Office.

Where should I go to request a correction?

Go to the Local Civil Registry (LCR) where the birth, marriage, or death was originally registered. Bring your supporting documents and identification for the correction process.

What are the types of errors that can be corrected?

  • Clerical Errors: Minor mistakes such as misspellings of names, wrong gender, or date typos.
  • Change of First Name: This requires a valid reason and supporting documents.
  • Legitimation or Adoption: Legal processes for changing the status or surname of a child.

What documents are required to file for a correction?

The required documents vary depending on the type of correction but may include:

  • Original copy of the PSA document with error
  • Valid government-issued ID
  • Supporting documents (e.g., school records, baptismal certificate, employment records)
  • Affidavit of Discrepancy
  • Court order (for certain corrections)

How long does it take to correct an error?

The timeline depends on the type of correction and the processing time at your local civil registry. Simple clerical errors may take a few weeks, while legal corrections may take several months, especially those requiring court orders.

Will PSA automatically update their records after the correction?

Yes, but only after the corrected record is transmitted by the Local Civil Registry to PSA. You may request an updated copy of your PSA certificate after a few weeks or months, depending on when the PSA receives and digitizes the corrected record.

Can I still use my PSA document even if it contains an error?

It depends on the purpose. Minor clerical errors may be accepted with an affidavit of discrepancy, but for legal purposes (passport, marriage, etc.), you may be required to have the correction processed first.
