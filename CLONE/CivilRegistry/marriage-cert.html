PSA Marriage Certificate

Overview of the PSA Marriage Certificate


A PSA Marriage Certificate is an official document issued by the Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA) that serves as legal proof of a couple’s marriage. It becomes a vital record once your civil status changes to “married,” and is often required for a wide range of legal and personal transactions.

This certificate may be needed when applying for joint bank accounts, processing insurance claims and pension benefits, or renewing your passport through the Department of Foreign Affairs (DFA). It is also used to support visa applications, property transactions, and other government-related processes.

Whether you prefer the ease of online services or wish to apply in person, it is important to understand the proper steps for obtaining your PSA Marriage Certificate. From submitting your request to receiving your copy, this guide outlines both online and walk-in application options, payment procedures, and delivery timelines to ensure a smooth and stress-free experience.

Who Can Apply

From submitting your request to receiving your copy, this guide outlines both online and walk-in application options, payment procedures, and delivery timelines to ensure a smooth and stress-free experience.

(Ikaw o ang iyong awtorisadong kinatawan ay maaaring humiling ng PSA Marriage Certificate sa pinakamalapit na PSA CRS Outlet. Pagkatapos mairehistro ang kasal sa LCRO, magiging available ang PSA Marriage Certificate sa loob ng 2 hanggang 3 buwan.)


Primary Requirements

  • One Valid Government‑Issued ID
    Required from the requester (and document owner if different). Accepted IDs include:
    • Philippine National ID / ePhilID
    • Philippine Passport
    • Driver’s License (LTO)
    • PRC ID
    • SSS / GSIS UMID
    • Voter’s ID (COMELEC)
    • Postal ID
    • Senior Citizen ID
    • OFW / OWWA ID
    • Seaman’s Book (MARINA)
    • Diplomat / Consular ID
    • NBI / Police Clearance
    • DSWD 4Ps ID / Certification
    • Barangay ID with photo and signature
    • PWD ID
    • IDs from National Government Offices or LGUs
    • TIN Card with photo and signature
    • School/Student ID (18+ currently enrolled students)
    • Company ID (from BSP/SEC/IC‑registered institutions)
  • For foreign nationals:
    Passport and one of the following:
    • ACR I‑card
    • Immigrant Certificate of Registration
    • Special Resident Retiree Visa (PRA)
  • Authorization Letter (if applying for someone else)
    Required unless you are:
    • The spouse of the document owner
    • A direct descendant
    • A legal guardian

Walk‑In Application Procedure

  1. Book an appointment via the PSA Online Appointment System.
  2. Go to your selected PSA CRS Outlet (e.g., Marikina) on your scheduled date/time.
  3. Present your CRS Appointment Slip (printed or digital) to the Information Marshal.
  4. Receive your Application Form (AF) and Queue Ticket Number (QTN).
  5. At the window, submit:
    • Filled-out AF
    • Valid IDs
    • Authorization Letter / SPA (if applicable)
    • QTN and payment
  6. Collect your Official Receipt (OR) and count your change, if any.
  7. Return on your scheduled release date, presenting:
    • OR
    • Valid IDs
    • Authorization Letter / SPA (if applicable)
  8. Verify that your document is correct and complete.

Processing Time and Fees

  • Fee: ₱155.00 per copy
  • Availability: 2–3 months after registration with the LCRO

Important Reminders

  • Arrive 30 minutes early for your appointment.
  • Ensure all IDs are valid and names match correctly.
  • Appointment booking is free and non‑transferable.
  • Authorized representatives cannot delegate authority.
  • Each representative may only request for 2 unrelated individuals.
  • Minors are not allowed to book appointments.
  • Transact only with authorized PSA or eSertipiko Marikina personnel.

PSA Frequently Asked Questions


When can newly married couples in Marikina apply for a PSA Marriage Certificate?


Couples are advised to request their PSA Marriage Certificate only after the official posting period. For marriages registered within Marikina City, the posting period typically takes about one to two months. If the PSA copy is not yet available, you may obtain a certified copy directly from the Marikina City Civil Registry Office where the marriage was recorded.

(Maaaring mag-request ng PSA Marriage Certificate ang bagong kasal matapos ang opisyal na posting period. Para sa mga kasal sa Lungsod ng Marikina, karaniwang tumatagal ng isa hanggang dalawang buwan ang posting period. Kung wala pa sa PSA ang record, maaari kayong kumuha ng certified copy mula sa Marikina City Civil Registry Office kung saan ito nairehistro.)


Who is allowed to request a PSA Marriage Certificate?


A Marriage Certificate may be requested by either spouse, their parents, children, a legally authorized representative, or a court of law.

(Maaaring humiling ng PSA Marriage Certificate ang alinman sa mag-asawa, kanilang mga magulang, anak, legal na kinatawan, o sa pamamagitan ng utos ng korte.)


What should I do if there is a spelling error in my name or my spouse’s name on the Marriage Certificate?



For corrections such as misspellings, you must file a petition for correction of entries at the Local Civil Registry Office (LCRO) in Marikina where the marriage was registered. Please note that corresponding fees may apply upon filing.

(Kung may maling baybay sa pangalan mo o ng iyong asawa, maghain ng petisyon para sa correction of entries sa LCRO ng Marikina kung saan nairehistro ang kasal. Mangyaring tandaan na may kalakip na bayarin sa pagsumite ng petisyon.)


Can I request delivery of my PSA Marriage Certificate outside the Philippines?


At the moment, delivery services are available only within the Philippines. For those residing abroad, you may authorize someone in Marikina to file and receive the document on your behalf, following proper authorization procedures.

(Sa ngayon, ang delivery ng PSA Marriage Certificate ay limitado lamang sa loob ng Pilipinas. Kung nasa ibang bansa ka, maaari kang magbigay ng authorization sa isang tao sa Marikina upang humiling at tumanggap ng dokumento para sa iyo, ayon sa tamang proseso ng awtorisasyon.)


What should I do if my Marriage Certificate is lost or damaged?


If your Marriage Certificate is lost or damaged, you can apply for a replacement copy from the PSA by submitting a request either online or at the PSA office. You will need to provide details of the marriage and valid identification.

(Kung ang iyong Marriage Certificate ay nawala o nasira, maaari kang humiling ng kapalit na kopya mula sa PSA sa pamamagitan ng online request o personal na pagpunta sa opisina. Kailangan mong ibigay ang detalye ng kasal at magpakita ng valid ID.)
