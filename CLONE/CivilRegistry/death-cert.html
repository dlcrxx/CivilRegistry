PSA Death Certificate

Overview of the PSA Death Certificate


A PSA Death Certificate is an essential document needed to manage the affairs of a deceased loved one. Issued by the Philippine Statistics Authority, this certificate serves as the official legal proof of a person’s passing and is required for a variety of important legal and administrative processes.

Whether you are arranging a funeral, claiming insurance or pension benefits, or settling an estate, the PSA Death Certificate is a crucial document to have. It validates the death and supports various legal and financial transactions related to the deceased.

While obtaining a PSA Death Certificate may seem overwhelming during a difficult time, the process is straightforward. This guide will help you understand how to apply for a PSA Death Certificate online, including step-by-step instructions for both online and walk-in applications. We’ll also cover important details about delivery options and how to securely receive your certificate.

With this information, you can confidently and efficiently secure the necessary document to assist you in handling the final affairs of your loved one.


Documentary Requirements for PSA Death Certificate Application

(Walk-In Application via eSertipiko Marikina)


Who Can Apply

You may request a Death Certificate at the nearest PSA CRS Outlet. This certificate officially records the date and cause of a person’s death. Processing is usually same-day, but applicants must follow the official procedure, including setting an online appointment.

(Maaari kang humiling ng Death Certificate sa pinakamalapit na PSA CRS Outlet. Ang sertipikong ito ay opisyal na nagtatala ng petsa at sanhi ng pagkamatay ng isang tao. Kadalasang makukuha ito sa parehong araw, ngunit kailangang sundin ang tamang proseso, kabilang ang pag-set ng online appointment.)


Primary Requirements

  • One Valid Government‑Issued ID
    Required from the requester (and document owner if different). Accepted IDs include:
    • Philippine National ID / ePhilID
    • Philippine Passport
    • Driver’s License (LTO)
    • PRC ID
    • SSS / GSIS UMID
    • Voter’s ID (COMELEC)
    • Postal ID
    • Senior Citizen ID
    • OFW / OWWA ID
    • Seaman’s Book (MARINA)
    • Diplomat / Consular ID
    • NBI / Police Clearance
    • DSWD 4Ps ID / Certification
    • Barangay ID with photo and signature
    • PWD ID
    • IDs from National Government Offices or LGUs
    • TIN Card with photo and signature
    • School/Student ID (18+ currently enrolled students)
    • Company ID (from BSP/SEC/IC‑registered institutions)
  • For foreign nationals:
    Passport and one of the following:
    • ACR I‑card
    • Immigrant Certificate of Registration
    • Special Resident Retiree Visa (PRA)
  • Authorization Letter (if applying for someone else)
    A signed authorization letter is required if the requester is not the document owner.

Walk‑In Application Procedure

  1. Book an appointment via the PSA Online Appointment System.
  2. Go to your selected PSA CRS Outlet (e.g., Marikina) on your scheduled date/time.
  3. Present your CRS Appointment Slip (printed or digital) to the Information Marshal.
  4. Receive your Application Form (AF) and Queue Ticket Number (QTN).
  5. At the window, submit:
    • Filled-out AF
    • Valid IDs
    • Authorization Letter / SPA (if applicable)
    • QTN and payment
  6. Collect your Official Receipt (OR) and count your change, if any.
  7. Return on your scheduled release date, presenting:
    • OR
    • Valid IDs
    • Authorization Letter / SPA (if applicable)
  8. Verify that your document is correct and complete.

Processing Time and Fees

  • Fee: ₱155.00 per copy
  • Availability: Processing time varies; follow the scheduled release date on your receipt.

Important Reminders

  • Arrive 30 minutes early for your appointment.
  • Ensure all IDs are valid and names match correctly.
  • Appointment booking is free and non‑transferable.
  • Authorized representatives cannot delegate authority.
  • Each representative may only request for 2 unrelated individuals.
  • Minors are not allowed to book appointments.
  • Transact only with authorized PSA or eSertipiko Marikina personnel.

PSA Frequently Asked Questions


How do I correct an error in the deceased’s first name on the Death Certificate?


You or your authorized representative can file a petition for correction under Republic Act 9048. Eligible petitioners include the deceased’s spouse, children, parents, siblings, grandparents, guardians, or other authorized persons. The petition can be filed at the Local Civil Registry Office (LCRO) where the death was registered, or through a migrant petition at any LCRO. Since death records are public documents, errors should be corrected promptly.


(Ikaw o ang iyong awtorisadong kinatawan ay maaaring maghain ng petisyon para sa pagwawasto ng impormasyon sa ilalim ng Republic Act 9048. Maaaring maghain ang asawa, anak, magulang, kapatid, lolo’t lola, tagapag-alaga, o iba pang awtorisadong tao. Ihain ang petisyon sa Local Civil Registry Office (LCRO) kung saan nakarehistro ang pagkamatay, o sa alinmang LCRO kung migrant petition. Dahil pampublikong dokumento ang death certificate, dapat itong maitama agad.)


I requested a Death Certificate but received a Negative Certification instead. What does this mean?


A Negative Certification indicates that the death record is not yet in the PSA archives. You can present this certification to the LCRO where the death was originally registered to have them endorse and forward the record to the PSA.


(Ang Negative Certification ay nangangahulugang wala pa ang death record sa archives ng PSA. Dalhin ang sertipikong ito sa LCRO kung saan unang nairehistro ang pagkamatay upang maipasa ito sa PSA para sa encoding.)


Does a Death Certificate expire?


No, a PSA Death Certificate does not expire. It remains valid indefinitely and should be accepted for all government and private transactions regardless of when it was issued.


(Hindi nag-e-expire ang PSA Death Certificate. Ito ay laging tanggap sa mga transaksyong pampubliko at pribado kahit kailan ito na-isyu.)


Can I request a Death Certificate for a relative who passed away many years ago?


Yes, you can request a PSA Death Certificate regardless of how long ago the death occurred. Just provide as much information as possible about the deceased, such as full name, date, and place of death, to help locate the record.


(Oo, maaari kang humiling ng PSA Death Certificate kahit matagal na ang pagkamatay. Ibigay lamang ang kumpletong detalye gaya ng buong pangalan, petsa, at lugar ng pagkamatay para madaling mahanap ang rekord.)


Is it possible to get a Death Certificate urgently?


Yes, some PSA partner centers offer expedited processing for Death Certificates for an additional fee. Check with the specific service provider for available rush options and estimated turnaround times.


(Oo, may ilang partner centers ng PSA na nag-aalok ng mas mabilis na pagproseso ng Death Certificate kapalit ng karagdagang bayad. Makipag-ugnayan sa provider para sa mga rush options at oras ng paglabas ng dokumento.)


Can I update or add information to a Death Certificate after it has been issued?


Yes, if there are missing details or errors on the Death Certificate, you can request an amendment by filing a petition at the Local Civil Registry Office (LCRO) where the death was originally recorded. Supporting documents may be required.


(Oo, kung may kulang o maling impormasyon sa Death Certificate, maaari kang humiling ng amendment sa pamamagitan ng petisyon sa LCRO kung saan unang nairehistro ang pagkamatay. Maaaring kailanganin ng mga sumusuportang dokumento.)
